Required parts for setup:

  • Laserdisc player, preferably with an easily accessable row of test points - all later Pioneers have that. I currently use the CLD-V2800, but have used an LD-V8000 with soldered on leads in the past.

  • Linux box with a PCI slot, preferably with a lot of CPU power and a few hundred GB of space. I use an i7 2600 personally, and encoding currently is at 5% real time. (But ideally that only needs to be done once per disk...)

  • PCI capture card with an CX23880 chip, running my cxadc-linux3 driver. Other SDR capture systems that can do 8fsc (28.6mhz) should also work, and the code can be adapted to work with other capture rates, ~25-26mhz minimum.

  • Highly recommended: signal amplifier. VGA amplifiers with adjustable gain such as the Ocean Matrix/Kramer OMX-4000 are suitable. There's another one that gives me 1-2 more Db, but I was unable to obtain another one.
